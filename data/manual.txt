Maintenance Guide

General Rules
Service interval should not exceed 30 days.
If service overdue, perform full inspection.
If multiple parameters exceed limits, stop machine immediately.
Always check lubrication before operation.

Temperature Rules
High temperature may indicate lubrication problem.
Very high temperature may indicate cooling failure.
Overheating may damage bearings and spindle.
If temperature remains high after service, inspect motor and cooling system.

Vibration Rules
High vibration may indicate bearing wear.
High vibration may indicate loose mounting.
High vibration during cutting may indicate tool imbalance.
If vibration increases with speed, inspect spindle bearings.
If vibration and noise both high, check alignment.

Noise Rules
High noise may indicate shaft misalignment.
Grinding noise may indicate bearing damage.
Knocking noise may indicate loose components.
If noise increases with load, inspect motor and coupling.

Lubrication Rules
Poor lubrication may cause overheating.
Low lubrication may damage bearings.
Dirty lubrication may cause vibration.
If lubrication status poor, clean and refill system.

Coolant Rules
Low coolant may cause overheating.
Coolant leakage may damage machine.
Blocked coolant line may cause temperature rise.
If coolant level low, refill and check pump.

Pressure Rules (Compressor / Pump)
Low pressure may indicate leakage.
Pressure drop may indicate valve problem.
High pressure may indicate blockage.
If pressure unstable, inspect regulator and valves.

Compressor Rules
If pressure low and temperature high, inspect compressor valves.
Oil leakage may indicate seal damage.
High noise in compressor may indicate piston wear.
If compressor overheats, check lubrication and cooling.

CNC Machine Rules
Unstable spindle speed may indicate motor issue.
Spindle noise may indicate bearing damage.
High vibration during cutting may indicate tool problem.
Poor surface finish may indicate misalignment.
If spindle speed fluctuates, inspect drive system.

Turbine Rules
High temperature in turbine may indicate cooling failure.
High vibration in turbine may indicate imbalance.
Delayed startup may indicate control issue.
If turbine overheats, stop operation immediately.

Safety Rules
If vibration high + temperature high → high risk.
If temperature high + lubrication poor → critical risk.
If service overdue + vibration high → failure risk.
If multiple warnings present → stop machine.
